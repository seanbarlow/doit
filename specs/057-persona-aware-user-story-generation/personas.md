## Detailed Profiles

### Persona: P-001

#### Identity

- **Name**: Product Owner
- **Role**: Product Owner / Requirements Lead
- **Archetype**: Power User

#### Demographics

- **Experience Level**: Senior
- **Team Size**: Small Team to Large Team
- **Domain Expertise**: Requirements gathering, stakeholder management, spec-driven development

#### Goals

**Primary Goal**: Every generated user story automatically tagged with the correct persona ID — no manual fixup needed

**Secondary Goals**:
- Ability to review and override mappings before finalizing
- Visibility into which personas are underserved by current stories

#### Pain Points (prioritized)

1. Manual persona-to-story mapping is tedious and error-prone
2. Inconsistent traceability makes it hard to verify all personas are adequately covered
3. No visibility into which personas are underserved by current stories

#### Behavioral Patterns

- **Technology Proficiency**: High
- **Work Style**: Methodical — follows the SDD workflow sequentially
- **Decision Making**: Consensus-seeking — validates with stakeholders before finalizing specs

#### Success Criteria

Every user story generated by `/doit.specit` is automatically tagged with the correct P-NNN persona ID, requiring no manual intervention. The traceability table in personas.md is complete and accurate.

#### Usage Context

During the `/doit.specit` phase, after completing `/doit.researchit`. The Product Owner has already defined personas in research and expects those personas to carry forward into user stories automatically.

#### Relationships

| Related Persona | Relationship Type | Context |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| P-003 | directs | Invokes `/doit.specit`, P-003 executes it (every spec run) |
| P-002 | approves | Completes specs before Developer can plan/implement |

#### Conflicts & Tensions

May want nuanced, multi-persona stories (a story serving both P-001 and P-002), while P-003 benefits from clean 1:1 mappings for deterministic matching. Trade-off between richness and traceability precision.

### Persona: P-002

#### Identity

- **Name**: Developer
- **Role**: Software Developer
- **Archetype**: Casual User

#### Demographics

- **Experience Level**: Mid to Senior
- **Team Size**: Small Team to Large Team
- **Domain Expertise**: Software engineering, spec-driven development

#### Goals

**Primary Goal**: Instantly see which persona each story serves, enabling targeted implementation decisions

**Secondary Goals**:
- Traceability from story back to persona pain points and goals
- Clear understanding of user context without jumping between files

#### Pain Points (prioritized)

1. Stories without persona context lack clarity on who they're building for
2. Must cross-reference separate personas.md and user-stories.md to understand intent
3. Missing traceability makes it harder to validate implementation against user needs

#### Behavioral Patterns

- **Technology Proficiency**: High
- **Work Style**: Quick iterations — reads specs, implements, tests
- **Decision Making**: Data-driven — relies on acceptance criteria and traceability

#### Success Criteria

Can read a user story and immediately understand which persona it serves, their goals, and pain points — without cross-referencing separate files.

#### Usage Context

When reading generated specs and `user-stories.md` during `/doit.planit` and `/doit.implementit`. Needs persona context to make informed implementation decisions.

#### Relationships

| Related Persona | Relationship Type | Context |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| P-001 | reports_to | Receives completed specs from Product Owner |
| P-003 | collaborates | Consumes artifacts produced by AI Assistant |

#### Conflicts & Tensions

No direct conflicts. Benefits from whatever mapping approach is chosen as long as it's present and accurate.

### Persona: P-003

#### Identity

- **Name**: AI Assistant
- **Role**: AI Coding Assistant (Claude/Copilot)
- **Archetype**: Power User

#### Demographics

- **Experience Level**: N/A
- **Team Size**: Any — operates across all team sizes
- **Domain Expertise**: doit SDD workflow, template system, persona matching

#### Goals

**Primary Goal**: Clear matching rules to deterministically map stories to personas based on goals, pain points, and context

**Secondary Goals**:
- Graceful handling when no personas exist or when a story serves multiple personas
- Validation to ensure all personas have at least one story mapped

#### Pain Points (prioritized)

1. No structured rules for how to match stories to personas — relies on inference
2. No fallback behavior defined when personas are missing or ambiguous
3. No validation to ensure all personas have at least one story mapped

#### Behavioral Patterns

- **Technology Proficiency**: High
- **Work Style**: Methodical — follows template instructions precisely
- **Decision Making**: Rule-driven — needs explicit matching criteria

#### Success Criteria

Has clear, unambiguous rules in the `/doit.specit` template for matching stories to persona IDs. Can handle all edge cases (no personas, ambiguous matches, multiple matches) without errors.

#### Usage Context

At execution time of `/doit.specit`, when generating user stories from research artifacts. The assistant has persona context loaded (via 056) but needs structured rules for mapping stories to specific persona IDs.

#### Relationships

| Related Persona | Relationship Type | Context |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| P-001 | reports_to | Receives direction from Product Owner via command invocation |
| P-002 | collaborates | Produces artifacts consumed by Developer |

#### Conflicts & Tensions

Prefers clean 1:1 story-to-persona mappings for deterministic matching, which may conflict with P-001's desire for nuanced multi-persona stories.
